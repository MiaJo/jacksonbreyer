  • automatic author identification (github repo)

    • Can we determine who wrote a novel, just by analyzing its writing style?

    That's the question I set out to answer when I began this NLP project. I used a corpus of novels from Gutenberg.org, some of which I tagged with their author, some of which I left untagged. I featurized all of the novels and used a Maximum Entropy Classifier to identify the authors of the untagged novels. Essentially, each tagged author became a class, and I sorted the untagged novels into one of these classes.

    What makes our writing unique? The trick was choosing the correct features. To speed up the runtime, I explored several possibilities using the bag-of-words model. To my surprise, the best feature was the relative frequency of the most frequent words (MFW).

    Take an example where my very most frequent word (MFW_1) is 'the', appearing 30% of the time. My second most frequent word (MFW_2) is 'of', appearing 10% of the time. Then we can say that their relative frequency is 0.3 - 0.1 = 0.2. So our feature MFW_1-MFW_2 would be set to 0.2. These relationships were very powerful when generalized to an author's 5 most frequent words.
